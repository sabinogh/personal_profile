Production Management

Project Overview

The project aims to inform you about everything in the factory, analyze the number of hours worked, and control factory expenses.

In this example, they were separated into three tabs: Shutdown Types, Monthly Costs, and Cost Trees.

Shutdown Types

We can see that the factory is separated into three classifications: Unproductive, Production and Rework.

In July a big jump in Unproductive hours along with Rework.

Causes:

A special project in the factory caused many design errors to be discovered when the equipment was delivered.

Solution:

Analysis together with the factory and the vendors of the project type for future improvements. After alignment meetings, we saw a drastic drop in Rework and Unproductive over the months. Consequently, factory Production had an increase in its indexes.

Result:

Aim for more productive working hours over the months.

Monthly Cost

Here you can get a better view of factory expenses. The company's cost is added to the notes coming directly from the factory by an ERP system. It uses the employee's hours x the machine operated, thus obtaining the factory cost.

In this project, you can see the expense by production categories and we know that the average annual cost of the factory is R$99,550.

Cost Tree

At this stage, we can analyze where the factory's costs are being consumed.

Looking at the "Unproductive" classification (where the greatest reduction efforts are), you can see the type of Resource (machine or work position), Employee and we arrive at the end, which is the Process.

In this case, the Process had the highest cost in "Lack of Supply". What does this mean? The operator spent a lot of time having to refuel the machine himself.

Solution:

Given the high manufacturing costs, it is clear that it is worth hiring a helper at the factory, who will have a lower hiring cost than the one generated by the Unproductive. By having the helper, you can meet all the "Supply Shortages" and free up the operator to produce more.
